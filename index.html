About

Every translation is an interpretation: choosing one word or phrase over another invariably chooses one meaning, one aesthetic, over another. By offering several translations of a literary work in parallel, ParallelLit allows you to access all these interpretations at once, so that you can better understand the original.

ParallelLit contains multiple translations for each text within its database and allows you to compare them side by side. Translations can be uploaded and compiled to help you better understand certain words, sentences, and selected passages.

Please lend us your feedback!

Sappho 31

φαίνεταί μοι κῆνος ἴσος θέοισιν ἔμμεν' ὤνηρ, ὄττις ἐνάντιός τοι ἰσδάνει καὶ πλάσιον ἆδυ φωνεί- σας ὐπακούει καὶ γελαίσας ἰμέροεν, τό μ' ἦ μὰν καρδίαν ἐν στήθεσιν ἐπτόαισεν·

Equal to Jove that youth must be — Greater than Jove he seems to me — Who, free from Jealousy’s alarms, Securely views thy matchless charms. Ah! Lesbia! though ’tis death to me, I cannot choose but look on thee; But, at the sight, my senses fly, I needs must gaze, but, gazing, die;

Lord Byron, 1820
